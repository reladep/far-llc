The Problem

Finding the right advisor shouldn't require a law degree.

Opaque fee structures

Fee disclosures are buried in dense legal filings. Most investors never know the full cost until it's too late.

No standardized performance data

Advisors self-report returns. Without a common standard, comparisons are meaningless — and often misleading.

Marketing masks credentials

Anyone can claim expertise. Actual regulatory filings reveal the truth about experience, client base, and disciplinary history.

Hidden conflicts of interest

Broker relationships, referral fees, and dual registration conflicts are disclosed in ADV filings — but rarely surfaced to clients.
