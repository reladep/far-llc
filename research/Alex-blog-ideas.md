## Running List

### 1. A Study of Uninvestable Assets
Explore the software selloff through the lens of recent "uninvestable sectors":
- European Debt crisis
- Energy in 2016/2017
- China overhang

Explore the negative sentiment around software today through this lens. Compare the starting points of valuation, and how easy/hard each one was to manage through.

---

### 2. Is Deflation Coming
Quantitatively explore what the world would look like if information services / knowledge work got disrupted:
- Who works in these jobs?
- Are they concentrated?
- What are the obvious knock-on economic effects?

Are there any asymmetric ways to play this, or is it an all-out short? Focus on high valuation, high market beta, high earnings exposure to these sectors.

---

### 3. Own Your Own Compute
Is the rise of OpenClaw a blip in a great rotation back to the personal server room?
- Running LLMs locally seems way more superior to the cloud for both consumers and enterprise
- Tokens are a b*tch
- If models are commoditized, why pay for API pings when there's a breakeven with a Mac Studio?

Maybe this is why memory stocks have run so much. Danger here is the cyclicality of semiconductors — other ways to play the bottleneck.
